Arriving

Most visitors will arrive via the small airport that serves all of Taniti, where small jets and propeller planes can be accomodated. Work to expand the airport to support commercial travel will complete in the next few years.

Cruise ships dock once a week at Yellow Leaf Bay.

Electricity

Electrical outlets are 120 volts, the same as the United States.

Language

English is commonly spoken by younger Tanitians. However, very little English is spoken by older residents, and in rural areas.

Medical Care

The island is serviced by one hospital, and many local clinics. Many hospital employees are bilingual.

Alcohol

The drinking age in Taniti is 18, and alcohol is not served between the hours of midnight and 9 A.M.

Safety

Taniti is a very safe country, with low rates of violent crime. However, as tourism increases, petty crimes like pickpocketing have become more common.

Holidays

Taniti enjoys a large number of national holidays, and many tourist attractions and restaurants will be closed on holidays, so visitors should plan accordingly.

Currency

Taniti uses the US dollar is it's currency, but Euros and Yen are commonly accepted. Banks are available to facilitate currency enchange, and many business accept credit cards.
